# 3-Day Weight Cut Plan for Boxing (68kg → 66kg)

## Overview
You need to lose **2 kg (4.4 lbs)** in 3 days while maintaining strength and performance. This is aggressive but achievable for a trained athlete.

---

## Daily Nutrition Plan

### Caloric Intake
- **Target: 1,800-2,000 calories/day** (significant deficit while training)
- Prioritize protein to preserve muscle during the cut

### Macronutrient Targets
| Macro | Daily Target |
|-------|------------|
| **Protein** | 130-140g (high priority) |
| **Carbs** | 150-180g (timed around training) |
| **Fat** | 40-50g (essential fats only) |

---

## Sample Daily Meal Structure

### Breakfast (7am)
- 3 egg whites + 1 whole egg
- 50g oatmeal (dry weight)
- Black coffee

### Mid-Morning Snack (10am)
- Protein shake: 30g whey protein + water + banana

### Lunch (1pm)
- 150g grilled chicken breast
- 150g white rice (cooked)
- Steamed broccoli (unlimited)

### Pre-Training (3:30pm)
- 40g rice cakes or white bread
- 15g honey

### Post-Training (5:30pm)
- 40g whey protein isolate
- 60g white rice cakes
- Electrolyte drink

### Dinner (7:30pm)
- 150g lean fish (cod, tilapia)
- 150g sweet potato
- Green vegetables

---

## Hydration & Fluid Management

### Days 1-2
- **Normal hydration**: 2.5-3L water daily
- Include electrolytes (sodium, potassium)

### Day 3 (Weigh-in Day)
- Reduce water intake 12 hours before weigh-in
- Light sip only if absolutely necessary
- **Rehydrate immediately after weigh-in** (isotonic sports drink)

---

## Training Considerations

| Day | Intensity | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **Day 1** | Normal training | Maintain intensity; adequate carbs |
| **Day 2** | Light-moderate | Technical work, avoid excessive cardio |
| **Day 3** (Weigh-in) | Rest or very light | Save energy for competition |

---

## Critical Do's & Don'ts

✅ **DO:**
- Eat frequent small meals (prevents hunger & muscle loss)
- Prioritize white carbs (easier to digest)
- Train hard enough to maintain performance
- Sleep 8+ hours per night
- Use electrolyte tablets/sports drinks

❌ **DON'T:**
- Skip meals (causes muscle breakdown)
- Eat high fiber foods (bloating)
- Do excessive cardio (muscle loss risk)
- Cut sodium completely (electrolyte imbalance)
- Dehydrate heavily before competition

---

## Post-Weigh-In Recovery (Critical!)
Within 1 hour of weigh-in:
- 500-800ml isotonic drink
- 30-40g carbs + 15-20g protein
- Gradually increase food intake over 3-4 hours before competition

---

**Important:** This is an aggressive cut. Monitor how you feel—if experiencing severe fatigue, dizziness, or weakness, adjust slightly upward. Consult your coach or sports nutritionist for real-time adjustments.
